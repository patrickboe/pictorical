My Motivation

I built this site because my wife is always like, "I really better take some pictures of the mural on such and such corner of Philadelphia, because you never know when they're just going to tear something like that down, and I've always liked it." I realized that there are already probably plenty of photos of that corner, geotagged with that location, publicly available on sites like Flickr, Panoramio, and Picasa.

In fact, in the years since Flickr first started supporting geotagging, they've amassed what amounts to a recent photographic history of many small places in the world. We should spend time enjoying this accomplishment. Using the information that Flickr and Panoramio publish about these public images, I put together this historical slideshow application. It shows a slideshow of the most "interesting" public photos in an area, from oldest to newest. At its best, it's a visual survey of a place's history. I think it makes for some fun viewing. While I was testing it, the images that popped up reminded me of places in my city that I love. I hope you will find the same things.

Links

Slideshow links on this site are unique. When you are viewing a slideshow, you can copy the link in the address bar and bookmark it or share it. The photos in the slideshow may change over time, as people add and remove photos from that location.

The Images

All the images displayed on this site are provided by their owners for public viewing on any site.

In the case of Flickr images, this site only displays images marked "public" and carrying a license which allows for reuse with attribution. Images on Flickr that are publicly accessible but marked "All Rights Reserved" (the default) are excluded from this application. So, if your images are on Flickr and you want them to show up in these slideshows, you must apply some form of license, such as one of the Creative Commons licenses. If your Flickr images are showing up on this site and you want them removed, you can do this in one of three ways:

  1. Remove the permissive license from your photo in Flickr, marking it "All Rights Reserved" instead. You can still keep it public. This will remove the photo from all compliant third-party applications, including this one. It may take a few hours for this to kick in.
  2. Remove the geotagging information from your photos on the Flickr website by going to Actions→Edit location on your map→Remove this photo from the map. This will remove your photos from all applications that use geotagging to find images.
  3. If you want to keep your current license and leave your photos geotagged, but have only this application updated to specifically exclude your images, you may submit your Flickr username to the application blacklist here.

In the case of Panoramio images, this site displays all public images, per the rights granted to third party sites like this one in Panoramio's user agreement.
